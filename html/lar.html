Introduction

Lost and Recovered is a first person adventure story game about a curious scientist exploring an abandoned military base.

This was my first game project and it made me fall in love with making games. It gave me a lot of challanges that I needed to overcome but it also taught me a lot about modeling, animating, game desing and writing c# code. My brother (TheCryingName) made the soundtracks for the game and everything else was done by me.

Modeling

Layout

The layout consists of 3 primary rooms interconnected by a central hallway. The player is challenged to figure out how to access all of the rooms, adding an element of puzzle-solving to the gameplay.

Modular Assets

All repeated 3D assets, including the walls, are designed to be modular, to help with the customization and decoration of the current level. Furthermore, these modular assets can be reused in subsequent levels, ensuring efficiency and consistency in level design across the game.

Mechanical Creature

This scene also features a mechanical spider-like creature that is used in the ending cutscene of this game demo.

Environment

Lighting

Most of the lights in the scene are baked, including the eerie glow from the green liquid-filled pipes. Real-time lights are only used when needed, such as for the ceiling light to showcase the volumetric fog effect.

Cutscene animation

Sparks Particles

I used the particle system to create an interesting particle effects for the malfunctioning wires of the door, ensuring that the sparking sounds are synchronized correctly to the visuals.

Dust Particles

Despite the scene looking good, it was lacking something - dust. I created a straightforward particle system for the dust particles, making sure they enhance the scene without overpowering it, while still being noticeable.
