Introduction

Potraga za Obrazom is a third person action-advanture game that follows the story of three high school friends from Podgorica who engage in heists together.

This game is a passion project of my brother and me, it came about from late night ramblings of my brother about his school trip where he came up with the Grujica Locksmith character. Later on we made a story around on Grujica, Nabi and Pero and that’s what I based my game around. My brother (TheCryingName) made sound tracks for the game and we even recorded rap songs on top of those tracks that can be found in game. He also helped me record all of the dialogue for the game. Game design, modeling, animation, scripting and everything else was done by me.

Character Design

Character Concept

Grujica, Nabi, and Pero are the three main characters of "Potraga za Obrazom". They are high school friends who enjoy engaging in mischief around the town of Podgorica in their free time. Grujica serves as the mastermind of their operations, possessing proficiency in lockpicking. Nabi, while grappling with substance-related challenges, showcases his expertise in handling electronics. Together, they make a good team, but they rely on Pero to ensure that no witnesses are left behind. Despite not being the brightest, Pero is the by far the most unhinged, willing to use unconventional methods such as throwing plates at anyone who comes in the way of their objective.

Environment

Modeling

Location of the game is set in the downtown area of Podgorica, particularly around Independence Square, where I spent a lot of time growing up. To ensure accuracy, I used Google Earth projections as a references for the proportions of the streets and buildings. Environment consists of before mentioned downtown area of Podgorica and 3 interior locations.

Environment in Unity

After transfering the models to Unity and configuring the lighting, the scene was ready. I used HDRP with volumetric fog, along with a combination of baked-in and real-time lights to achieve the desired visual effects.

Light Optimization

In order to manage the performance impact of numerous street lights in the scene while maintaining the volumetric fog effect, I implemented an optimization strategy. The lights were baked into the scene and a cone was used with a shader to simulate the volumetric effect. When the player approaches the light, the real-time light activates, allowing the player to observe the genuine volumetric effect when the camera is positioned under the light.

Cars

The game was looking a bit lifeless, so a straightforward system for spawning cars was added. The system involves randomly selecting a car model, car sound, spawn time, and a preset path for the car to follow. Additionally, the cars are programmed to detect when a player is in their path and they sound their horn. If a player fails to move out of the way they can get run over.

Tutorial

Tutorial System

The tutorial system aims to seamlessly teach players about the game's controls and mechanics throughout the gameplay, fostering a more immersive and informative gaming experience. Players learn about the game mechanics as they come across them, with explanations provided in a straightforward and non-disruptive manner.

Main Menu

Interactive Buttons

In the main menu, each button is linked to an object in the background. Upon hovering over or pressing a button, the associated object dynamically responds with an animation.

Music Player

The music menu opens up a mobile phone with a music player where you can listen to the full version of the songs you’ve encountered in game. Initially, these songs are locked and can only be played once you complete specific missions.
